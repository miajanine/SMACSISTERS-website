Project Zen: About SMAC

SMAC Profile and Data


The Sorelle Missionarie dell'Amore di Cristo, Inc (SMAC) is an orphanage founded and run by the nuns of the Missionary Sisters of the Love of Christ. Months prior to the founding of the institution in 1998, they observed the poor living conditions of the people in their parish, the children that were abused, alone, and had no-one to turn to. They witnessed the families unable to care for their sick children, let alone provide for their growing needs.

Based on research conducted, we have gathered that the SMAC foundation is in need of a more sustainable source of income. Although they have benefactors, volunteers, and even sponsors for each child, it is still a struggle to keep up with all the expenses needed for the upkeep of everything in the center. It is seen especially in the data collated from the interview with SMAC wherein 9 out of the 10 children didn't receive the psychological assessment as per requested by their professional because they were short on money even with discounts available. This is ultimately crucial knowledge because it notifies us that this is the specific issue we should be aiming to solve.

We gathered this data through extensive research and background checking of the partner institution. We looked into the various websites published online about the foundation as well as the interview conducted by Ma'am Karen Pido with the head of the foundation, Sister Luzviminda Pepito. In these, we could clearly see the foundation's most outstanding problem, lack of funding, in more ways than one. Mainly, not being able to pay for facility bills, medical treatment of the kids, and more.

The SMAC Ins. relies heavily on its benefactors and sponsors financially and because of that it causes a decline of how much support the SMAC Ins can give towards the children. Having knowledge of this data is important as we continue our research because it gives us a better understanding of the institution of how we can provide aid and help towards it. It helps us brainstorm ideas on how we can support the institution such as creating a promotional facebook page for the institution and donating things such as books, notebooks, pens, and clothes. As the SMAC Ins. heavily relies on benefactors and sponsors, having known of this data can help guide us on what we need to do.

One thing we found interesting about SMAC is how they do everything for these kids, like schooling them, nurturing them, raising them, and taking care of them without looking for any profit. Considering how much they work for these kids, it's surprising how they do this all for free. It makes sense, however, as these kids don't have anything that could be used as "payment" for this group. Because of this, they've been giving more than they get, and are losing money. This is why we're taking initiative and giving them a branch to make more money to support the children they care for.

We ensured that the data gathered were reliable and valid by checking its sources. Specifically who made, published, and released it to the public. In our case, this information was released by the teachers from Ateneo de Davao University Junior High School in collaboration with the SMAC institution making it a credible source of information.

Pananaw

Ang isyu na gusto namin bigyan ng pansin ay ang kanilang kakulangan ng pundo para sa mga kailangan nila. Masasabi namin ito dahil ang aming pananaliksik ginamit katulad sa video tungkol sa SMAC institusyon, sinasabi nila na hindi sila suportado ng gobyerno, at umaasa sila sa mga padrino ng bata at sa mga pabor. Ito ay dapat maayos dahil ang ibang problema nila ay kaugnay sa problema naito. Pag maayos ang problema naito, ang ibang isyu nila ay mawala katulad sa kakulangan sa edukasyon at mapaayos rin ang kanilang kalusugang pangkaisipan.

Swerte kami na di namin nararanasan ang problema hinaharap ng aming institution. Ngunit meron kaming mga kilala na may problema na tulad ng mayroon ang institution. Halimbawa nito ay and aking Tita na nawalan ng trabaho sa panahon ng pandemya. Napilitan siya na ibenta ang bahay niya upang magkaroon ng pera at mabuhay habang wala pa siyang trabaho. Tulad ng SMAC, siya ay dumaan ng maraming pagsusubok at kahirapan dahil sa problemang ito. Kaya naman, ang mungkahi namin para maayos ang suliranin ng institution ay magbibigay kami ng paraan para magkaroon ng mga gulay upang siyang makain at mabenta. Magagawa namin ito sa pamamagitan ng aming SIP kung saan kami ay nananaliksik ng mga paraan upang maparami at mapaganda ang kalabasan ng iba’t -ibang gulay at halaman. Ang papel na ito ay maari nilang magamit sa pagtatanim nila ng mga possibleng produkto. Higit pa rito, konti lamang ang mga tindahan malapit sa institusyon, dahil sa ito satingin namin malakas ang kita nila sa negosyo lalo na dahil mga gulay ang benta.

Makatulong ito dahil sila ay maka tipid sa pagbili ng gulay at makakuha rin sila ng kita sa pag benta ng natitirang gulay. Sa panahon na matagumpay ang mungkahi na ito, sila ay makahukuha ng sapat na pondo upang mabili ang mga kakukulangan nila. Hindi na nila kailangan mag-asa sa mga donasyon at pabor ng iba. Kung mamatuloy ito pwede nila mapaunlad ang kanilang gusali, edukasyon, at ang pamumuhay nila. Mas lalo pa namin ito mapapanindigan kung tutulong kami sa pagsimula ng negosyong ito. Sigurado ako na kung ipapakita namin ang amin umaapaw na suporta sa kanila, ay magtatagumpay at malayo ang mararating nila.

The common illnesses of our kids are cough, allergies, and running nose. Also, they have a low immune system which causes them to infect each other whenever one of them catches a cold.


​ Livelihood-Source of the Institution
We train our Kidson bread making, juice making, baking, and cooking of Filipino snacks or kakanins.
For the institution, our main source of funds is coming from both local and international funding. However, the sisters-in-charge are always struggling to make ends meet. The pandemic has worsened our conditions because it has crippled the livelihood of most of our sponsors as well. A practical example is our telephone line might be cut off due to missed payment (by July of 2020).


​ Socio-Cultural Information
Mostly our Kids are Bisaya-speaking and Ilonggo (Hiligaynon speakers). Our Kids are also trained to speak the local language, Filipino and English.


​ State of the Environment
We try our best to teach the children how to disposed of the wastes accordingly but often times the children cannot maintain the cleanliness of our home.


​ Peace and Order
So far our home is located in a peaceful place. Although our neighborhood is well known for petty thieves. Mintal Police Station is the nearest authority we can approach. Our Barangay Tanod and Purok Leader help us maintain the peace also.


​ Existing Organizations involved in the Institution
Ateneo de Davao University Junior High School Community - students for their regular program, parents, and faculty.


​ Records of Past Interventions
Repainting of the toys and recreational area, making fence by the AdDU Junior High and Irish students from Ireland.
